# Accounts Payable (AP)

## What is Accounts Payable?
Accounts Payable (AP) represents short-term obligations owed to suppliers for goods/services received but not yet paid. It is a current liability on the balance sheet.

Journal entry when purchasing on credit:
  Dr. Inventory/Expense
  Cr. Accounts Payable

Journal entry when paying:
  Dr. Accounts Payable
  Cr. Cash/Bank

## AP Process (Procure-to-Pay)
1. Purchase Requisition
2. Purchase Order (PO) sent to supplier
3. Goods Receipt and inspection
4. Invoice Receipt from supplier
5. Three-Way Matching: PO vs Goods Receipt vs Invoice
6. Invoice Approval by finance team
7. Payment Execution per agreed terms
8. Ledger Update

## AP Aging Report
Categorizes outstanding payables by age: 0-30 days, 31-60 days, 61-90 days, 90+ days.

## Key Metrics
- Days Payable Outstanding (DPO) = (AP / COGS) x 365
- AP Turnover Ratio = Total Purchases / Average AP

## Best Practices
- Segregate duties: approver and payment releaser should be different people
- Require POs for all purchases above threshold
- Use automated AP software to prevent duplicate payments
- Reconcile AP sub-ledger with general ledger monthly
- Maintain verified vendor master file
- Negotiate longer payment terms to improve working capital
