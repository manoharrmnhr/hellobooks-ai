# Accounts Receivable (AR)

## What is Accounts Receivable?
Accounts Receivable (AR) represents money owed to a business by customers for goods/services delivered but not yet paid. It is a current asset on the balance sheet.

Journal entry on credit sale:
  Dr. Accounts Receivable
  Cr. Revenue

Journal entry on cash collection:
  Dr. Cash/Bank
  Cr. Accounts Receivable

## AR Process (Order-to-Cash)
1. Sales Order placed by customer
2. Delivery or service rendered
3. Invoice issued to customer
4. Payment follow-up and reminders
5. Cash Application: payment matched to invoice
6. Dispute Resolution for short-payments
7. Ledger Update

## AR Aging Report
- Current (0-30 days): Normal
- 31-60 days: Send reminder
- 61-90 days: Follow-up call
- 90+ days: Legal or collections

## Bad Debt Management
Allowance for Doubtful Accounts reserves an estimate for uncollectible receivables.
  Dr. Bad Debt Expense
  Cr. Allowance for Doubtful Accounts

## Key Metrics
- Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) = (AR / Revenue) x 365
- AR Turnover Ratio = Net Revenue / Average AR
- Collection Effectiveness Index = Collected / (Beginning AR + Sales) x 100
- Bad Debt Ratio = Bad Debt Expense / Revenue x 100

## Best Practices
- Conduct credit checks before extending credit
- Set clear payment terms in contracts and on invoices
- Send automated reminders at 7, 3, and 1 day before due
- Offer early payment discounts (e.g., 1% for payment in 10 days)
- Review aging report weekly
- Consider AR factoring to improve immediate cash flow
