# Invoices

## What is an Invoice?
An invoice is a commercial document issued by a seller to a buyer, detailing goods or services provided, quantities, agreed prices, and payment terms. It serves as a formal request for payment.

## Types of Invoices
- Standard Invoice: Common type issued after delivery, requesting payment by due date.
- Pro Forma Invoice: Preliminary estimate invoice, not a demand for payment.
- Credit Invoice (Credit Note): Reduces amount owed — used for returns or billing errors.
- Debit Invoice (Debit Note): Increases amount owed for additional charges.
- Recurring Invoice: Auto-generated at regular intervals for subscriptions or retainers.

## Required Invoice Components
1. Invoice Number – Unique sequential identifier.
2. Invoice Date – Date issued.
3. Due Date – Payment deadline (e.g., Net 30 = due 30 days after invoice date).
4. Seller Information – Name, address, GSTIN.
5. Buyer Information – Client name, address.
6. Line Items – Description, quantity, unit price, subtotal.
7. Subtotal – Total before taxes.
8. Tax Details – GST/VAT rates and amounts.
9. Total Amount Due.
10. Payment Terms and Methods.

## Common Payment Terms
- Net 7: Due within 7 days
- Net 30: Due within 30 days
- Net 60: Due within 60 days
- 2/10 Net 30: 2% discount if paid within 10 days, otherwise due in 30
- Due on Receipt: Immediate payment expected

## Invoice Lifecycle
Draft → Sent → Viewed → Partial → Paid → Overdue → Void

## Accounting Journal Entries
On invoice issuance:
  Dr. Accounts Receivable
  Cr. Revenue

On payment receipt:
  Dr. Cash/Bank
  Cr. Accounts Receivable

## GST Invoice Requirements (India)
- GSTIN of supplier and recipient
- Place of supply
- HSN/SAC codes
- CGST, SGST, or IGST breakdown

## Best Practices
- Number invoices sequentially and never reuse numbers
- Send invoices promptly after delivery
- Follow up with automated reminders at 7, 14, and 30 days overdue
- Store all invoices digitally for at least 7 years
