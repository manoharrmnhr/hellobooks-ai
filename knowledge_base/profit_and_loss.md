# Profit and Loss Statement (Income Statement)

## What is a P&L Statement?
The Profit and Loss (P&L) statement summarizes a company's revenues, costs, and expenses over a specific period (monthly, quarterly, annually). It shows whether the business made a profit or incurred a loss.

## P&L Structure

### Revenue (Top Line)
- Gross Revenue: Total sales before deductions
- Less: Returns and Allowances
- Net Revenue = Gross Revenue minus Returns

### Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
Direct costs of producing goods or services:
- Raw materials, direct labor, manufacturing overhead

Gross Profit = Net Revenue minus COGS

### Operating Expenses (OPEX)
Indirect costs: salaries, rent, marketing, depreciation, software subscriptions.

Operating Profit (EBIT) = Gross Profit minus Operating Expenses

### Non-Operating Items
Interest income/expense, asset sale gains/losses, forex gains/losses.

Profit Before Tax (PBT) = EBIT plus/minus Non-Operating Items

### Tax
Corporate income tax and deferred tax adjustments.

Net Profit (Bottom Line) = PBT minus Tax

## Key P&L Ratios
- Gross Margin = Gross Profit / Revenue x 100
- Operating Margin = Operating Profit / Revenue x 100
- Net Profit Margin = Net Profit / Revenue x 100
- EBITDA Margin = EBITDA / Revenue x 100

## Common Mistakes
- Mixing cash and accrual entries inconsistently
- Forgetting to record depreciation
- Classifying capital expenses as operating expenses
- Omitting non-cash items like stock-based compensation
